Nikola Tesla

The visionary inventor who revolutionized electrical power and wireless communication

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856: Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, in Smiljan, now part of Croatia.
  • 1884: Tesla arrived in New York City with little more than a letter of recommendation for Thomas Edison, leading to his early work in the U.S.
  • 1887: Tesla patented his induction motor and began promoting alternating current (AC) power systems.
  • 1888: Tesla entered into a contract with George Westinghouse, who adopted Tesla’s AC system and made significant investments in its development.
  • 1891: Tesla invented the Tesla coil, a device used to generate high-voltage, high-frequency alternating current.
  • 1893: Tesla demonstrated wireless lighting and power transmission at the World’s Columbian Exposition in Chicago, showcasing his AC technology.
  • 1895: Tesla’s AC system was used to generate electricity at the Niagara Falls Power Plant, marking a significant milestone in electrical engineering.
  • 1901: Tesla began construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower on Long Island, intended for wireless communication and power transmission.
  • 1904: Tesla was granted a patent for a radio transmitter, which laid the groundwork for future radio technology.
  • 1915: Tesla was nominated for the Nobel Prize in Physics, alongside Thomas Edison, for his contributions to the development of electrical engineering.
  • 1920: Tesla published various papers and gave lectures on wireless power transmission, discussing potential applications for global energy distribution.
  • 1943: Nikola Tesla passed away on January 7 in New York City, leaving behind a legacy of groundbreaking inventions and contributions to science and technology.
